Riverine Drought

The user can impose a drought on either the Colorado River or the Salt and Verde Rivers, or both. The current controls are defined as: 1) No Drought, 2) Abnormally Dry, 3) Moderate, and 4) Extreme.

Definitions:

Abnormally Dry; the current drought stops in 2020 and the flows (starting in 2013) are reduced by 5% on the Colorado River and 15% on the Salt-Verde Rivers,

Moderate; the current drougth stops in 2030. Flows on the Colorado River are reduced by 15% during this period. Flows on the Salt-Verde Rivers are reduced by 30%.

Extreme; the current drough stops in 2040, with flows on the Colorado river decreased by 40% while flows on the Salt-Verde Rivers are reduced by 65% during this period.

Field name: DROUSCEN
