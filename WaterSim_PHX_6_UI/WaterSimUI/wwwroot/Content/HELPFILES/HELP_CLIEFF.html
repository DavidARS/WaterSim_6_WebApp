Predefined Climate/River Flows

The user can set a predefined climate effect on river flows for the Colorado River and the Salt-Verde Rvers; the current controls are defined as: 1) Flood, 2) No Effect, 2) Slight, 3) Moderate, and 4) Severe.

Definitions:

Flood: Increased flows on all rivers by 20% above historical (120% of historical, for the river trace used).

No Effect: No reduction in river flows.

Slight: Flows are reduced by 11% on the Colorado River and 20% on the Salt-Verde Rivers,

Moderate: Flows on the Colorado River are reduced by 30% while flows on the Salt-Verde Rivers are reduced by 35%.

Severe: Flows on the Colorado river are reduced by 40% while flows on the Salt-Verde Rivers are reduced by 45%. This setting mimics the RCP 8.5 emission pathway.

NOTE: The river "trace" (the 30-year segment from the historical flow-gauge-record) changes with the predefined settings; we use a low flow record for the Moderate and Severe choices and the Median flow record for the Slight setting.
